Section 02 — Market Context

The Great Reclassification

Why the U.S. Government Just Made Your Degree More Valuable

In Q4 2024, the Department of Labor reclassified 147 job categories, creating unprecedented demand for credentialed business leadership. This regulatory shift has fundamentally altered the executive education landscape.

Three Critical Changes

1. The "AI Manager" Designation

New federal guidelines now require formal business education for roles managing AI-augmented teams. Over 340,000 positions were reclassified in 2025, with MBA or equivalent becoming the baseline credential.

2. Executive Compensation Bands

Fortune 1000 companies are restructuring compensation frameworks to align with new labor classifications. MBA-holders now qualify for Band 3+ positions, representing a $45K-$85K average premium.

3. Remote Work Reclassification

Distributed team leaders now fall under "Strategic Manager" classifications, requiring documented business strategy education. This affects 2.3M roles projected by end of 2026.

The Bottom Line

These regulatory changes create a 24-36 month window where credentialed executives will capture outsized salary growth. Early movers in 2026 are positioned to benefit from supply-demand imbalances before the market normalizes.
